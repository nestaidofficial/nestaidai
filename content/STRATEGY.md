# Nestaid SEO Content Strategy — 90-day plan

**Goal:** rank Nestaid on page 1 for the top 20 buyer-intent keywords in the home care operations space, and own the passage-level citations that AI search (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT web, Perplexity) use to answer home care agency questions.

**Starting position (April 2026):** fresh domain, 0 backlinks, 0 indexed content pages, 8 product/company pages, clean technical SEO foundation, GA4 + GSC + Rich Results verified.

**Success signals at day 90:**

- 8–12 indexed blog posts
- 5+ non-branded organic keywords ranking in top 30
- Branded search ("nestaid") at #1
- 2–4 natural backlinks from home care forums / LinkedIn shares
- Consistent AI Overview appearances for "AI receptionist home care" class of queries

---

## Who we're writing for

Three concrete personas. Every post should be written for one of them.

| Persona | Role | Pain point | Primary intent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rita, Agency Owner | Owner of 1–3 location home care agency (50–200 caregivers) | Can't scale ops without hiring more coordinators | "How do I grow without drowning in operational chaos?" |
| Marcus, Scheduling Coordinator | Frontline coordinator | 6 AM call-out scramble, 3 PM shift-change chaos | "How do I fill this shift right now?" |
| Priya, Agency Director of Operations | Ops leader at 3+ location agency | KPIs on fill-rate, EVV compliance, caregiver retention | "What tools reduce ops cost and risk?" |

Don't write for "home care professionals" generically. Pick a persona per post.

## Content requirements (non-negotiables)

Every post must have:

- [ ] Single H1 matching the title
- [ ] Meta description under 160 chars with the primary keyword in the first half
- [ ] One clear persona target
- [ ] At least one table or structured comparison (AI Overviews reward these)
- [ ] Passage-level optimization (every H2 answerable as a standalone snippet)
- [ ] 2+ internal links (one to pillar page, one to a product page)
- [ ] 1 outbound link to an authoritative source (stats, regulations, etc.)
- [ ] Concrete numbers in at least 3 places (ROI math, stats, benchmarks)
- [ ] CTA at the bottom linking to `/pricing` or the Calendly demo link
- [ ] `keywords` frontmatter populated (already supported by `src/lib/blog.ts`)

---

## Internal linking strategy

- Every cluster post links **UP** to its pillar page (anchor text = primary keyword).
- Pillar page links **DOWN** to every cluster post it owns.
- Product pages (`/management`, `/scheduling`, `/ai-onboarding`) link **laterally** to the matching pillar.
- Homepage FAQ answers link to the specific blog post that expands on the answer.

**Rule:** no orphan pages. Every post is reachable from at least 2 other pages.

## Competitive positioning (how to beat incumbents)

Primary competitors: CareSmartz360, AlayaCare, AxisCare, HHAeXchange, WellSky.

They all have content, but most of it:

1. Is generic / not buyer-intent focused
2. Doesn't mention AI
3. Reads like brochures
4. Has no actual operational playbooks

**Nestaid's angle:**

- Be the **AI-native** voice in home care ops (they are still on "digital transformation" messaging)
- Be the **practical playbook** source — concrete ROI math, real coordinator workflows, actual numbers
- Be the **passage-level citation** target — write in a way that AI Overviews can pull verbatim
- Be **faster** — publish weekly while incumbents publish monthly

## Distribution (don't skip this)

The post is 30% of the work. The other 70% is getting it seen.

For every post, do:

1. Post to LinkedIn (both founders' accounts) with a thread summarizing 3 insights
2. Share in 2 home care operator Facebook groups / forums
3. Reply to relevant Reddit threads in r/homehealthcare (only when genuinely helpful — not spammy)
4. Answer 1 Quora question with a link back
5. Email the post to any agency owners in your network
6. Request GSC indexing for the new URL

A $0 distribution loop is worth more than any ad spend at this stage.
